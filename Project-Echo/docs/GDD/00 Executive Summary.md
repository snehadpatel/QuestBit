# Executive Summary

## Purpose

This document provides a concise executive overview of Project Echo for leadership, production, and cross-functional partners.

## Scope

Covers the product concept, target audience, strategic positioning, and release direction.

## Dependencies

- The game must remain centered on communication-driven co-op horror.
- Scope must remain compatible with a small independent studio production plan.

## Diagrams

## Examples

- A session where players must compare inconsistent realities to solve a single objective.
- A match that creates a memorable story through a communication failure rather than a scripted event.

## Edge Cases

- The concept may be misunderstood by players expecting action-focused horror.
- A weak vertical slice may make the product look derivative.

## Design Decisions

- The project is positioned as a communication-first experience rather than a conventional monster game.
- The MVP focuses on one facility and one creature model to reduce risk.

## Future Improvements

- Add facility variety and deeper narrative content.
- Expand creature behaviors and player progression.

## Risks

- Over-scoping could undermine the quality of the core loop.
- Marketing could fail if the game is not clearly differentiated.

## Open Questions

- Which feature is the clearest proof of the game’s identity for the vertical slice?
- What pricing and launch strategy best support the Steam audience?
